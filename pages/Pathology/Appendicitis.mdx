# Acute Appendicitis

<details>
  <summary>Definition</summary>
  Appendiceal inflammation ranging from oedema to ischaemic necrosis and perforation
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Clinical Features</summary>
  1️⃣ Umbilical Pain --> Localised Pain in RIF 2️⃣ Anorexia 3️⃣ Nausea (not vomitting)
  4️⃣ Diarrhoea (in retrocaecal appendix)
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Special Signs</summary>
  1️⃣ Rovsing's Sign = Pressure in LIF -> More pain in RIF 2️⃣ Psoas Sign = Pain on
  extending hip
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Investigations</summary>
  1️⃣ Principally a clinical diagnosis 2️⃣ Bloods: FBC, CRP, amylase, G+S, clotting
  3️⃣ Urine: Exclude pregnancy and UTI 4️⃣ Imaging: CT can be used; US for excluding
  gynae pathologies 5️⃣ Diagnostic lap
</details>

<details>
  <summary>Management</summary>
  1️⃣ Fluids 2️⃣ **Abx**: cef 1.5g + met 500g IV TDS 3️⃣ **Analgesia**: Paracetamol,
  NSAIDs, codeine phosphate 4️⃣ Certain Dx → appendicectomy (**lap** > open)
</details>
